National Animal

Tiger

Tigers are the national animal of India.

A tiger is a type of large cat that is found in Asia and southern Russia. The scientific name for a tiger is Panthera tigris. Tigers are the largest of the world's big cats. Male tigers can weigh up to 300 kilograms and measure nearly 13 feet (4 m) in length. The tiger is a carnivore and it preys on animals such as deer, bullocks, antelopes, etc. the female tiger is called the “tigress” and the offspring is the “cub”. Tigers live in “lairs”. They communicate with each other through roars and are recluse animals.

Despite their impressive features, tigers face significant threats to their survival. Habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and poaching for their bones, skin, and other body parts have led to a decline in tiger populations. Conservation efforts, including the establishment of protected areas and anti-poaching measures, are crucial for the preservation of these magnificent creatures. Tigers play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance by controlling herbivore populations. Efforts to raise awareness, enforce stricter conservation laws, and promote sustainable coexistence with tigers are essential for ensuring the long-term survival of this iconic species.
